# Human Owner Questions

## Business Model

- Is CourseForge intended to be sold first to individual instructors, freelance instructional designers, departments, or institutions?
- Is the first paid promise "export a Canvas package" or "generate/edit unlimited course drafts"?
- Should CourseForge remain Canvas-only for launch, or support other LMS platforms later?

## Target Customer

- Who is the first buyer with budget: instructor, instructional designer, department chair, school admin, or university procurement?
- Are K-12 schools in scope, or is the initial market higher education and continuing education?
- Should the app optimize for solo course builders or team review/approval workflows?

## Pricing

- Is the `$20/mo` dashboard price a real target or placeholder?
- Should export be the paid gate, or should AI generation volume be the paid gate?
- Will there be a free trial, free preview, one-time export purchase, or subscription only?
- Are institution plans expected at launch?

## Demo Strategy

- Should public demo mode use only deterministic generation to avoid AI cost?
- Should demo users be able to download `.imscc` packages?
- Should sample courses be prebuilt, generated live, or both?
- How explicitly should the UI label the current prototype as unverified for Canvas import?

## Branding

- Is CourseForge the final name?
- Should "Canvas Builder" remain in the product name, or become a subtitle?
- Should product language say "Canvas-ready," "Canvas-oriented," or "Canvas import package" before sandbox verification?

## User Roles

- What roles are required for launch: instructor, instructional designer, org admin, super admin, student, reviewer?
- Do students ever log into CourseForge, or only into Canvas after export?
- Do departments need shared workspaces and project ownership transfer?
- Who can export, who can edit, and who can manage billing?

## AI Behavior

- Which provider/model should power real generation?
- Should users approve a blueprint before detailed content generation?
- What source documents should the app parse first: PDF, DOCX, plain text, Canvas export, syllabus?
- Should AI regenerate whole courses, individual modules, or object-level sections?
- What AI use/cost limits should each plan have?

## Data Retention

- How long should uploaded source files be stored?
- Should generated projects have version history?
- Should deleted projects be recoverable?
- Do institutions require data deletion or retention controls?

## Billing

- Should Stripe be the first billing provider?
- Should billing be per user, per workspace, per export, per course, or usage-based?
- Who receives invoices for team plans?
- Should unpaid users keep edit access but lose export access?

## Security

- What data classification should uploaded syllabi and course materials have?
- Are FERPA or institution privacy requirements in scope?
- Should uploaded content be used for model training? If not, how should the UI state that?
- What HTML should be allowed in Canvas pages?

## Integrations

- Should the MVP remain `.imscc` export only, or add Canvas API publishing?
- Is LTI integration a future requirement?
- Does the product need Google Drive, OneDrive, or LMS import ingestion?
- Should exported packages include institution templates, policies, or branding?

## Launch Plan

- What counts as beta-ready: local package generation, successful Canvas import, or full SaaS accounts?
- How many Canvas sandbox imports must pass before marketing can say Canvas-ready?
- Who will provide the known-good Canvas export fixture?
- What analytics or support channels are needed before public traffic?
